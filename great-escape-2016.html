Citizens Across The U.S. Are Planning Their Great Escape This Election Season

As the battle for the White House rages on, candidates are dropping like flies, kind of. (Looking at you, Ben Carson.) Chances are, your pick might not be left in the race, and even if they are, might not make it to the Oval Office.

Not to fear, though. If you don't like something, there's no reason you should have to put up with it. So don't. Actualize everyone else's threats and just move away.

We've taken the liberty of putting together some destinations. Comment your own escape plan below.

The Obvious Choice: Canada

Canada has seen a surge in Americans seeking to cross the border in the past month. Follow the crowd and get yourself a nice place in Saskatchewan.

Pros: socialized health care (if you're into that sort of thing), great waterfall, everyone is super polite

Cons: Tim Horton's, very cold

Immigration website

The Tight Regime: Russia

If the presidency doesn't have enough power for you, go somewhere there's more. Find someone Donald Trump might find extreme -- Vladimir Putin. He won't let his citizens get into shenanigans, and besides, they're still riding the high of the Olympics.

Pros: Lots of vodka, very nice architecture

Cons: Less free speech, very cold

Immigration website

The Luxurious Getaway: Barbados

Located in the Caribbean, Barbados is surrounded by beautiful blue waters. It's got a representative democracy and is a popular destination for cruise ships.

Pros: Rihanna is from here, very warm

Cons: Widespread poverty, heavy reliance on tourism industry

Immigration website

The Wild Ride: Western Sahara

This country is just south of Morocco and is mostly populated by tribes that live by herding camels. It doesn't have a central government, so if you've just completely given up on politics, here's the place.

Pros: Do whatever you want, lots of camels

Cons: It's a desert, has Moroccan landmines everywhere, very hot

Immigration website: you can probably just show up
